So you got a free version of Sentinel and now you want to build a SOC.

  1. Documentation
    1. IP Adresses:
      1. Knowing all the IP addresses your company owns is a good start. You would be surprised how many companies don't even have that.
      2. Lists of known bad IP addresses can help with preventing known attacks.
    2. Assest Inventory
    3. Employee Directory with Job Description
    4. Spreadsheet Of Approved Apps And Duties By Job Title
  2. Clearly Defined Roles
    1. Who will make the decision when X happens?
      1. When a server is Hacked?
      2. When a server is Ransomwared?
      3. do you pull it off line?
      4. do you power it off?
      5. do you segregate it on the network and track the flow of data? (should already have tcpdump in place somewhere tracking the network activity, like on a firewall).
  3. Regular Back-Ups
  4. Playbooks and Processes
  5. Employee Training
    1. Browse Microsoft Certifications and Exams
    2. Become an Azure Sentinel Ninja: The complete level 400 training
  6. First Alert
    1. how to respond
    2. what to look for
    3. how to determine severity
    4. how to get more information about the alert. check the KQL code used by the alert to generate the alert.
  7. Hunting
    1. KQL
    2. Logs - figuring out what needs to be turned on for logging.
  8. Questions you should always be asking yourself
    1. How can I improve the process?
    2. What might go different than your plans?
  9. Things to consider
    1. Cost. Storage can become expensive if you go past the free 30 days allowed by Microsoft. You should consider what logs you want to collect/retain, how to retain them and for how long you should retain them.
    2. Employee knowledge.
      1. L1 little knowledge but stubornly good at learning things and is self motivated to learn more, knows when to ask for help. (Entry Level, notice I didn't mention a need for 5 certifcations and years of experience or a Ph D.)
      2. L2 Has some experience Good at learning and teaching, self motivated, knows when to ask for help.
      3. L3 Great at googling, more experience great at learning and teaching, self motivated, knows when to ask for help. Stays calm under stressful situations.
  10. Stay Current
